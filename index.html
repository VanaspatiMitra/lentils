About the Crop:

L-9 is a high-yielding variety known for its adaptability and nutrient-rich seeds. It thrives in well-drained, loamy to sandy loam soils with a pH of 6.0 to 7.5. The crop requires approximately 500-700 mm of water per acre, which can be supplied through rainfall or irrigation. Typically, L-9 yields about 600-800 kg per acre. Profit margins can be quite favorable, often ranging from 20-30%, depending on input costs and market prices.

  • Soil Composition: sandy to clay loamy soils with a pH range of 6.0 to 7.5
  • Water need per Acre: 1.64 to 2.30feet
  • Potential Dangers: Soil Degradation, Pest Infestation, Waterlogging
  • Fertilizers: Diammonium Phosphate, Organic Matter, Potassium, Nitrogen
  • Pesticides: Emamectin benzoate ,Thiamethoxam, Acetamiprid
